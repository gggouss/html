Führer

Adolf Hitler was the leader of the Nazi Party and became Chancellor of Germany in 1933, subsequently assuming the title of Führer in 1934 after the death of President Paul von Hindenburg. His leadership was marked by aggressive military expansion and policies that led to the outbreak of World War II. Hitler's regime had a profound impact on global history, resulting in significant political, economic, and social changes across Europe and beyond.
